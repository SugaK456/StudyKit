LCA of a Single-Serve Coffee Capsule System

Comparative and cradle-to-grave LCA of an aluminium coffee capsule system, from raw material extraction through end-of-life treatment.

▶
Scenario Description

A major Italian coffee brand is evaluating the environmental performance of its single-serve aluminium coffee capsule system. The goal of this exercise is to perform a cradle-to-grave LCA of the system, from the production of the capsule and its contents to end-of-life treatment.

The capsule is produced in a facility in Germany (DE). It is made of aluminium (3.2 g) and a polypropylene lid (0.5 g), sealed with a paper filter (0.3 g). The assembly of one capsule requires 0.8 Wh of medium-voltage electricity.

Each capsule contains 5.5 g of roasted and ground coffee. Coffee is grown and processed in Colombia, and the unitary impacts of 1 kg of roasted coffee are reported in Table 2. The coffee is transported from Colombia to Hamburg by container ship (10,450 km), then by small lorry (680 km) to the filling plant in Italy (IT).

The filled capsules are packed into cardboard boxes (10 capsules per box, box weight 48 g) at the Italian plant. The packaged product is transported by small lorry (220 km) to a distribution centre.

During the use phase, the consumer brews one capsule using a coffee machine consuming 0.033 kWh of electricity (low voltage, IT) per brewing cycle and 200 ml of tap water (from surface water – IT).

☛ Functional unit: the preparation and consumption of 1 cup of coffee (one capsule brewing cycle).

At end-of-life, two scenarios must be modelled. Reference scenario: capsule disposed of as MSW and sent to incineration with energy recovery (unitary impacts in Table 3). Alternative scenario: capsule separately collected for aluminium recycling via system expansion (85% Al recovery; collection requires 0.04 kWh/capsule of medium-voltage electricity).

Impact categories: "Climate change" and "Resource use, fossils" — EF 3.0 Method (adapted). See Tables 4 and 5 for elementary flows and providers.

LCA of a 2 MW Onshore Wind Turbine

Cradle-to-grave LCA of a 2 MW onshore wind turbine, assessing the environmental performance of wind electricity over its 20-year operational lifetime.

▶
Scenario Description

A renewable energy company based in Denmark (DK) is assessing the environmental performance of a 2 MW onshore wind turbine. The goal is a cradle-to-grave LCA covering raw material extraction and manufacturing, 20 years of electricity generation, and end-of-life treatment.

The turbine tower is manufactured in Germany (DE) from steel hot rolled coil (180,000 kg). The nacelle and hub assembly — also produced in Germany — contain cast iron (28,000 kg), copper wire (3,200 kg), and aluminium sheet (1,800 kg). The three rotor blades are produced in Denmark and are made of glass fibre reinforced polymer (each blade 6,500 kg; 70% glass fibre, 30% epoxy resin). The foundation uses concrete (320,000 kg) and reinforcing steel (18,000 kg), poured on-site.

All steel and nacelle components are transported from the German factory to Denmark by large lorry (680 km). Blades are transported by large lorry (95 km). Installation requires 45,000 kWh of medium-voltage electricity.

During its 20-year operational life, the turbine generates an average of 6,000 MWh per year (capacity factor ~34%). Maintenance requires 2,500 kg of lubricating oil over the lifetime and one gearbox replacement at year 10 (gearbox: 14,000 kg steel + 800 kg copper; unitary impacts in Table 2).

☛ Functional unit: 1 kWh of electricity delivered to the grid. All impacts divided by total lifetime electricity generation (20 yr × 6,000 MWh/yr = 120,000 MWh = 1.2 × 10⁸ kWh).

At EoL: steel and metals are recycled via system expansion (recycled steel displaces primary steel, 90% recovery). Blades are sent to landfill (unitary impacts in Table 3). Concrete is crushed for road aggregate (no burden, no credit).

Impact categories: "Climate change" and "Resource use, minerals and metals" — EF 3.0 Method (adapted). See Tables 4 and 5.
